The Container — What to Use
Use a large thermocol (expanded polystyrene / EPS) box — the kind used for fish transport or medicine cold storage. Size: roughly 40cm × 30cm × 25cm. Cost: ₹80–150 from any vegetable market or packaging store. DO NOT use cardboard (no insulation) or hard plastic (hard to drill holes). Thermocol = perfect prototype medicine container.

Which Box Should You Use?
✓ THERMOCOL BOX
Best choice. Naturally insulated. Easy to drill holes with a knife. Cheap (₹80–150). Looks exactly like real medicine transport. Available at vegetable markets, fish shops, packaging stores.
SIZE: 40×30×25 cm
~ PLASTIC CONTAINER
Works but not ideal. No insulation. Harder to drill cleanly. Use only if thermocol unavailable. A large Tupperware-type box with lid works. Need to add foam padding inside.
SIZE: 35×25×20 cm+
✗ CARDBOARD BOX
Don't use. No insulation = can't demonstrate temperature control. Gets soggy with humidity tests. Falls apart during vibration demo. Doesn't look professional.
NOT RECOMMENDED
★ PRO UPGRADE
Get a medical-grade foam vaccine carrier box (used for immunization drives) — available from pharmacy suppliers or NRHM stores. Pre-made, professional, real use case. Impresses judges instantly.

Step by Step Assembly
1
Day 1 — Hardware
Prepare the Container
Get a large thermocol box (~40×30×25 cm). Cut or melt 3 holes: one on the top for a flexible hose (air inlet for hot/humid air injection during demo), one on the side for wire routing, one small hole for the RFID antenna to scan from outside.
🔧Use a soldering iron tip or hot knife to cut clean holes in thermocol — don't use scissors.
📏Mark all hole positions with a marker before cutting. Measure twice.
🌬️The air inlet should fit a flexible dryer hose or PVC pipe — used to blow hot air from a hair dryer during temperature simulation demo.
2
Day 1 — Hardware
Mount Sensors on Inner Walls
Use double-sided foam tape or hot glue to fix sensor modules to the inner walls of the box. Left wall: SHT40, MPU6050, TSL2591. Right wall: BMP280, MQ-2, MAX30102. Top inner face: RFID RC522 (near the hole). Keep wires routed along the walls neatly using tape.
📍SHT40 must be near center of the box for accurate ambient reading — not right next to the air inlet hole.
💡TSL2591 should face the lid opening direction so it detects light when lid is opened.
⚡MQ-2 needs warmup time (~2 min) before accurate readings — note this in your demo script.
3
Day 1-2 — Hardware
Set Up ESP32 + Breadboard on Base
Place the breadboard on the base of the box (foam-safe tape). Mount ESP32 on the breadboard. Connect all I2C sensors first — they all share GPIO 21 (SDA) and GPIO 22 (SCL), so run a common I2C bus line along the inner walls. Then add SPI connections for RC522 and SD card module.

4
Day 2 — Hardware
Add Output Components
Mount OLED display on the front inner face of the lid (so it's visible when lid is open). Attach RGB LEDs and buzzer near the OLED. These are your physical alert indicators. Drill tiny holes through the lid edge so LEDs are visible from outside too.
💡Hot glue the OLED module directly to the inner lid face. The screen should face inward and be readable when lid is open at 90°.
🔴For LEDs visible from outside: push them through small holes in the lid edge, secure with hot glue from inside.
5
Day 2-3 — Firmware
Flash ESP32 Firmware
Write and upload firmware in this order: (1) Basic sensor reads — confirm all sensors respond on I2C scan. (2) Add MQTT publish. (3) Add SD card logging. (4) Add RFID profile loading. (5) Add alert logic + LED/buzzer control. (6) Add FFT tamper detection. Test each layer before adding the next.
🖥️Run i2c_scanner sketch first to confirm all I2C addresses are detected — this catches wiring errors early.
📡Use HiveMQ WebSocket client to test MQTT messages before building the dashboard.
💾Test SD card separately with a simple write/read sketch before integrating.
6
Day 3-4 — Software
Build the Dashboard
React + Firebase. Start with a single live temperature chart. Then add all sensors. Then add the safety score gauge. Then alert history panel. Then trip report PDF export. Driver alert tablet screen is a separate simple HTML page on a second device connected to same Firebase.
📊Use Chart.js or Recharts for sensor graphs. Firebase onValue listener gives you real-time updates without polling.
📱Driver alert screen: literally just a big-font HTML page on a tablet showing current status. Keep it simple — RED/YELLOW/GREEN + the alert message.
7
Day 4-5 — Demo Prep
Set Up Demo Scenarios
Prepare 4 demo scenarios that you'll run live: (1) Temperature rise — blow hot air from hair dryer through inlet hose. (2) Humidity rise — moist towel near inlet or spray water mist. (3) Light exposure — open the lid. (4) Vibration/tamper — shake the box sharply. Each should trigger alerts on dashboard within 5-10 seconds.
🌡️Rehearse the demo flow 3–4 times. Know exactly when each alert triggers and what to say at that moment.
📝Have a printed "medicine profile" card showing the RFID tag ID and its thresholds — visual prop that helps explain the RFID feature.
🔋Run fully on power bank during demo. No laptop charger cables running to the box — looks cleaner.

Zone A — Left Inner Wall
SHT40, MPU6050, TSL2591

Mount all three vertically with 2cm spacing. SHT40 at center height for best ambient reading. TSL2591 should face the lid direction. MPU6050 can be anywhere on the wall — it reads the whole box vibration regardless.
Zone B — Box Floor Center
ESP32, Breadboard, SD Card

Central position keeps wire lengths minimal to all wall sensors. Breadboard flat on the floor. ESP32 plugged into breadboard. SD card module on breadboard. Power bank sits beside the breadboard.
Zone C — Right Inner Wall
BMP280, MQ-2, INA219

BMP280 at top (pressure is uniform in box anyway). MQ-2 at mid-height. INA219 near where the cooler power cable runs — it's inline with the cooler's power supply wire, not just sitting there.
Zone D — Top Center (near hole)
RC522 RFID, MAX30102

RFID antenna should be near or flush with the small hole cut at the top of the box so tags can be scanned from outside. MAX30102 near top if monitoring container O₂ — or mount externally with a finger clip for driver HRV monitoring.
Zone E — Lid Inner Face
OLED, RGB LEDs, Buzzer

OLED faces inward. When lid is opened at demo, OLED is immediately visible. LEDs pushed through small holes at lid edge — visible from outside when box is closed. Buzzer mounted flat on lid inner face.
